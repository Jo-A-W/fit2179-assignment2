Chapter I

The Big Picture

Australia is one of the world's largest food exporters, sending over $66 billion worth of food products overseas in 2025. Over the past two decades, the value of Australia's food exports has more than doubled — driven by growing demand from Asia and rising global food prices.

"Australia exports enough food to feed approximately 75 million people annually — nearly three times its own population."
Chapter II

What Australia Exports

From beef to wheat, Australia exports a wide variety of food products to markets across Asia, the Middle East, and beyond. Beef consistently ranks as Australia's most valuable food export, followed by wheat and other grains.

Chapter III

State by State

Australia's agricultural output is spread across the continent, with each state specialising in different commodities. Western Australia dominates grain production, Queensland leads in beef, and Victoria is the dairy heartland.

Chapter IV

How Trade Has Shifted Over Time

Australia's trading relationships have shifted significantly over the past two decades. China has grown to become Australia's largest food export market, while wheat exports have surged in recent years driven by global supply shortages.

"While China and Japan are Australia's biggest food export markets, New Zealand and Singapore are actually net food import partners — Australia buys more food from them than it sells."
